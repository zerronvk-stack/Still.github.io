Still

Privacy Policy  ·  Effective 21 July 2026

Still does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. Not as a policy choice we could quietly reverse — as a property of how the app is built.

No network access at all

Still does not request the INTERNET permission. Android therefore prevents the app from making any network request whatsoever. There is no server, no API, no endpoint. You can verify this yourself by inspecting the app's permissions.

Your notifications never leave your phone

Still's digest feature uses Android's notification access to hold back notifications from apps you have chosen to defer, and return them to you later in a daily summary. That notification content is stored only in the app's private storage on your device, is never transmitted anywhere, and is used for no purpose other than showing you your own digest.

What we do not do

  • No analytics or telemetry SDKs of any kind.
  • No advertising, ad networks, or ad identifiers.
  • No accounts, sign-ins, email collection, or user profiles.
  • No location, contacts, camera, microphone, or file access.
  • No data sold, shared, or disclosed to third parties — there is no mechanism by which it could be.

Purchases

Still Pro is a one-time purchase processed entirely by Google Play. We never see, receive, or store your payment details. Your purchase is validated by the Google Play Billing service on your device. Google's handling of that transaction is governed by the Google Privacy Policy.

Your data, and deleting it

Everything Still stores — your settings, your quiet-time history, your milestones, your written moments — lives only on your device. Uninstalling Still permanently deletes all of it. There is nothing held elsewhere to request, export, or erase, because nothing ever left.

Children

Still is not directed at children under 13 and collects no data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the revised version will be posted at this address with an updated effective date. Any change that would involve collecting data would also require a new app permission, which Android would surface to you before it could take effect.
